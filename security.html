What is Internet Security?

Internet Security means protecting yourself, your data, and your devices when you use the internet. Just like you lock your house to keep thieves out, we need to protect our digital lives too.

Why Should You Care?

You share personal information online every day - photos, messages, location
Your phone contains bank details, family contacts, and private conversations
One mistake can cost you money, embarrassment, or worse
Cybercriminals target young people because they think you won't notice

Being smart online is as important as being smart in real life

Threat #1: Phishing

Fake messages that trick you into giving personal information

Email Phishing

Fake emails from "banks" asking for passwords

SMS Phishing

Text messages claiming you won a prize

Social Media

Fake accounts asking for personal details

Voice Calls

Calls claiming your account is blocked

Threat #2: Malware

Harmful software that can damage your device or steal information

1
Viruses: Corrupt your files and slow down your phone
2
Spyware: Secretly watches what you do online
3
Ransomware: Locks your files until you pay money
4
Adware: Shows unwanted ads everywhere

Password Security

Your first line of defense

1
Use at least 12 characters with numbers, symbols, and letters
2
Never use the same password for multiple accounts
3
Avoid personal information like birthdays or names
4
Use password managers like Google Password Manager
5
Change passwords if you suspect they're compromised

Two-Factor Authentication (2FA)

Add an extra layer of security to your accounts

99.9%

Reduction in Account Takeovers

2 Steps

Password + Phone/App Code

Enable 2FA on Instagram, WhatsApp, Gmail, and banking apps

Even if someone gets your password, they can't access your account

3
